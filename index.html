1968
Origins

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

Intel’s early innovations helped enable long-term performance-per-watt improvements in computing.

  • Company formation and growth
  • Innovation groundwork
  • Long-term industry impact
1971
Innovation

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

Smaller, more efficient computing systems became possible—setting the stage for future energy gains.

  • Computing miniaturization
  • Performance improvements
  • Efficiency pathways
1978
Architecture

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

x86 longevity enabled sustained innovation and efficiency gains across generations of hardware.

  • Standardized platform
  • Long-term compatibility
  • Scaling performance per watt
1985
Performance

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

More capability per chip helped reduce the need for multiple components, improving efficiency over time.

  • 32-bit architecture
  • Improved multitasking
  • Platform growth
2006
Emissions

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

Key shift from peak emissions toward sustained reductions through technology and cleaner electricity.

  • Chemical abatement investments
  • Renewable electricity scaling
  • Efficiency-focused manufacturing
2020
Climate
Water

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

RISE aligned goals across climate, water, and waste for measurable progress through 2030.

  • Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling
  • 2030 sustainability goals
  • Industry-wide collaboration
2022
Emissions

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

Set a long-term trajectory for operational decarbonization with ongoing efficiency and renewable efforts.

  • Scope 1 & 2 focus
  • Operational efficiency
  • Renewable scaling
2023
Energy
Emissions

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

Renewable electricity is a major lever for reducing operational emissions while scaling clean power globally.

  • 99% renewable electricity
  • Lower operational emissions
  • Scalable energy strategy
2024
Supply Chain

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

Scaling sustainability requires partner alignment—this summit focused on shared progress across the supply chain.

  • Supplier collaboration
  • Shared sustainability standards
  • Manufacturing innovation
